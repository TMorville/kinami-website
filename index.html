Drone Reporter

Turning Sightings Into Positions
The Problem

In September 2025, unidentified drones flew over Copenhagen Airport, Aalborg Air Base, and several military sites in Denmark. Police opened phone lines for the public to call in sightings, and thousands did. Most reports turned out to be stars, planes, or birds. There was no good way to tell the difference.

The Idea

Drone Reporter is a mobile app. You see something in the sky, point your phone at it, and tap. The app takes a photo and records where you are, which direction you're pointing, and at what angle. That's one observation. When two or more people report the same object, the system works out where it actually is in 3D space.

No phone calls, no guesswork. The data just gets better with each person who reports.

How It Works
01
Point and Report
Aim your phone at the object and take a photo. The app logs your GPS position, compass heading, and camera angle. Together, these define a line from you to whatever you're looking at.
02
Triangulate
Two or more sightings from different positions give intersecting lines. The intersection is a position: latitude, longitude, and altitude.
03
Track
Repeated triangulations over time build up a flight path. Authorities get live access through a REST API with positions, trajectories, confidence scores, and photos.

Audioreactive

Visuals That Listen
Concept

A visual instrument that turns music into art in real time. It picks up the weight of a kick drum, the shape of a melody, the texture of a hi-hat, and generates images that feel like the music looks.

Built for live settings. Plug into a venue's sound system or a DJ mixer and the visuals play out on stage with the music. Nothing is pre-rendered or manually triggered. It just responds to what it hears.

How It Works
01
Listen
A microphone or audio feed captures what's playing. The system breaks that down into rhythm, pitch, and energy about thirty times per second.
02
Interpret
Those features steer a generative model through image space. Low frequencies move the big shapes, mids shift composition, highs add texture.
03
Generate
The model produces a new image every frame. The output never repeats and stays musically coherent. It doesn't just flash on every beat. It actually follows the music.
